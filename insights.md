# Adventure Works Retail Analytics — Key Insights

A comprehensive analysis of Adventure Works' 2016–2017 retail data revealing revenue drivers, customer value concentration, geographic performance variations, and product profitability patterns.

---

## 1. Monthly Revenue Trends

From September 2016 to June 2017, revenue showed a clear upward trend, with June and May leading at $1.8 million and $1.7 million respectively. This growth indicates increasing customer demand, which directly translates to higher profits for the business. A growing company creates new opportunities — more jobs, higher wages, business expansion. Based on this pattern, I would recommend increasing production capacity in the first half of the year to meet anticipated demand.

**Key Metrics:**
- Peak Month: June 2017 ($1.83M)
- Consistent Growth: 9-month upward trajectory
- Customer Acquisition: ~2,000 new customers per month
- Average Order Value: $300–$340

---

## 2. Revenue by Product Category

Bikes have the highest price per unit ($1,698) and massive revenue although having the lowest margin with around 41%. Accessories had the lowest price per unit ($15) whereas the highest margin of 63%. Clothing was in the middle and lowest revenue. Based on the margin analysis, I'd recommend increasing investment in accessories to boost overall profitability, given the 63% profit margin compared to bikes' 41%.

**Key Metrics:**
| Category | Revenue | Profit | Margin | Units Sold |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Bikes | $23.6M | $9.7M | 41.14% | 13,929 |
| Accessories | $906K | $569K | 62.83% | 57,809 |
| Clothing | $365K | $161K | 44.27% | 12,436 |

**Insight:** Bikes drive volume, accessories drive margin. The opportunity lies in bundling high-margin accessories with every bike purchase.

## 4. Regional Performance

The United States and Australia emerged as our most profitable countries, outperforming all other markets by a significant margin. This growth was primarily driven by regional surges in Australia ($7.4 million) and the Southwest US ($4.8 million). Meanwhile, overall sales saw strong upward momentum across Australia, the Western US, and European markets. In stark contrast, the Southeast, Northeast, and Central US regions lagged severely behind, yielding a negligible combined profit of less than $22,000. Given these disparities, I'd recommend investigating why Southeast, Northeast, and Central regions underperform, while scaling investment in Australia, Southwest US, and Europe where demand is clearly strong.

## 5. Top Products by Revenue

The Mountain-200 series is our clear flagship product line, with all six variants dominating performance by generating over $500,000 in profit each at a peak margin of 46.03%. In contrast, profitability drops off sharply for the Road and Touring models, which see lower sales volumes and compressed margins ranging between 36.36% and 39.46%. To maximize future growth, the business should immediately double down on marketing and inventory support for the high-margin Mountain-200 line. Additionally, management should optimize production for the Road-150 Red variants, which show strong premium potential by generating impressive revenue despite lower unit sales. Finally, the lower-performing Road-350-W Yellow models should be bundled or discounted to quickly clear out slower-moving inventory.

## Conclusions

Adventure Works has built a strong, profitable business centered on premium bicycles with exceptional geographic performance in Australia. The data reveals:

1. **Revenue is growing** — consistent month-over-month increases indicate healthy market demand
2. **Profitability is concentrated** — bikes drive volume, accessories drive margins; bundling is key
3. **Customer value is concentrated** — top 10 customers represent significant revenue; retention is critical
4. **Geographic efficiency varies dramatically** — Australia outperforms by 3x per customer; US regions fragmented
5. **Product portfolio is narrow** — Mountain-200 dominates; this is both a strength (proven winner) and a risk (limited diversification)

The path forward is clear: **protect high-value customers, capitalize on bestselling products, optimize margins through bundling, and investigate geographic underperformance.**
